Model's Answer: Claude Monet indeed painted multiple versions of the "Japanese Bridge," also known as the "Water-Lily Pond" series. Monet's Japanese Bridge series was created in his garden at Giverny, France. One of the most famous versions is the one he painted in 1899. This particular version is often referred to as "The Japanese Bridge" and is housed in the collection of the National Gallery in London, United Kingdom.
